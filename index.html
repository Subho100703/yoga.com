Paschimottanasana
Paschimottanasana, or Seated Forward Bend, is a seated yoga pose that involves bending forward to touch your toes while keeping your legs straight. It stretches the spine, hamstrings, and shoulders, calms the mind, and stimulates the digestive system. To perform it, sit with legs extended, inhale to lengthen the spine while raising arms, then exhale to bend forward from the hips, reaching for your toes without forcing the stretch. üßò‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è
Ardha Matsyendrasana
Ardha Matsyendrasana, or the Half Lord of the Fishes Pose, is a seated spinal twist yoga posture known for improving spinal flexibility and health. Its benefits include stimulating digestion, relieving back pain and stiffness, and reducing stress. To perform it, sit with one leg bent and the foot outside the opposite hip, twist the torso to the bent leg side, and place the opposite hand on the knee while reaching the other arm behind your back. üåø
Bakasana
Bakasana, or Crane Pose, is an intermediate-to-advanced arm-balancing yoga pose where you balance on your hands with your knees on your upper arms.
